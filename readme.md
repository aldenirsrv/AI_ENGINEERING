# AI/LLM Engineering in Practice

> Small, focused projects. Production-grade mindset. Real engineering and architecture decisions.

A hands-on repository exploring the engineering and architecture of modern AI/ML systems — how they are designed, built, evaluated, and operationalized. Every project is scoped small but structured to reflect the decisions that matter at scale.

---

## Why This Exists

The AI ecosystem moves fast. Most content explains *what* things are. This repository focuses on *how* they are actually designed and built.

> **"How would this actually be architected and implemented in a real system?"**

That question drives every project here.

---

## Core Disciplines

### System Architecture
Every AI system involves architectural decisions that determine how components interact, how data flows, and how the system scales. Projects here explore pipeline design, component boundaries, and the tradeoffs behind different architectural patterns for LLM-based systems.

### Retrieval Engineering
Retrieval is one of the most critical — and most underestimated — layers of production AI. This area covers how information is indexed, retrieved, ranked, and delivered to a model accurately and efficiently, including the architectural decisions that make retrieval systems reliable at scale.

### Knowledge Representation
How knowledge is structured shapes what a system can reason over. This area explores knowledge graphs, entity relationships, and graph-based retrieval — architectural approaches that go beyond flat vector search into richer, more connected representations of information.

### Context Engineering
What enters the context window determines what the model can produce. This discipline covers document preparation, chunking strategies, metadata design, and retrieval optimization — the engineering layer that sits between raw data and the language model.

### Evaluation & Observability
A system without measurement cannot be trusted or improved. This area covers retrieval evaluation, hallucination detection, groundedness validation, and answer quality analysis — the feedback infrastructure that production AI systems depend on.

### Agentic Systems & Orchestration
How LLMs are composed with tools, memory, and multi-step reasoning. Projects here explore the architectural patterns behind agentic pipelines — how decisions are structured, how state is managed, and how control flows through a system.

### Applied Implementations
Domain-specific systems — ecommerce, telecom, internal policy, and others — where the above disciplines converge under real-world constraints. The focus is always on the engineering and architecture behind the implementation, not just the outcome.

---

## Engineering & Architecture Mindset

Every project, regardless of scope, is approached with the same set of principles:

- Clear architectural boundaries and separation of concerns
- Reproducible, observable, and measurable pipelines
- Evaluation built in from the start, not added at the end
- Grounded generation and hallucination control by design
- Code structured for adaptability, not just correctness

The goal is not to build demos. The goal is to understand how **reliable, well-architected AI systems** are actually engineered.

---

## Philosophy

Engineering clarity and architectural realism over polished productization.

Some projects are intentionally incomplete. Others exist purely to stress-test an architectural decision or validate a retrieval hypothesis. The value is in the understanding — not the finish line.
